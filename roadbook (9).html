How to use

① Load a GPX file
Drag and drop your GPX track file onto the drop zone, or click to browse. The app reads the track points and shows the total distance.
② Interpolation — Method
None (raw GPX) — uses your file exactly as recorded. Fine if your GPS logs frequently (every few metres).
Linear — inserts extra points in straight lines between existing ones for uniform spacing. Good for most tracks.
Spline — fits a smooth curve through your points before adding new ones. Best for sparse GPX files or winding roads.
Spacing
Target distance between points after interpolation. 10 m is a good balance — lower for more precision, higher for faster processing.
③ Turn detection — Min angle
Minimum direction change to count as a turn. Lower = more tulips (gentle bends included). Higher = only sharp turns. Start at 30° and adjust to taste.
Turn detection — Min dist
Minimum distance from the last turn before a new one is detected. Prevents duplicate tulips for the same corner. Raise if turns appear too close together.
④ Generate roadbook
Processes the GPX with your settings and builds the tulip table. Each row shows the total KM, the junction diagram, and a notes field.
⑤ Fetch surfaces
Queries OpenStreetMap (needs internet) for the road surface type near each turn. Result appears as a coloured hexagon in the bottom-right of each tulip:
■ Black = asphalt/paved   ■ Grey = gravel   ■ Brown = dirt/mud
■ Yellow = sand   ■ Green = grass   ■ White = ice/snow
Three hexagons in a triangle = loose surface. A red outer ring means difficult terrain.
⑥ Fetch elevation
Calculates steep slopes from your GPX elevation data (no internet needed). A red diagonal arrow appears next to the hexagon — pointing up-right for climbs, down-right for descents. Only shown when the grade is 6% or steeper.
⑦ Add signals (⊕ button)
Click the red ⊕ button in any notes cell to open the signal picker. Click a symbol to place it in the top-right corner of the tulip. Click a placed symbol to remove it.

! — Single warning   !! — Danger / double warning
● — General caution (yellow dot)
Bridge — Road goes over a bridge
River crossing — Water crossing ahead
Railroad — Level crossing ahead
Fuel stop — Fuel available
Waypoint — Passage control / checkpoint
Surface & slope symbols can also be placed manually here if OSM data is missing or outdated.
⑧ Notes field
Free text field per tulip row. Type any caution, instruction or landmark name. Notes are included in the PDF export.
⑨ Print / PDF
Opens a preview window with your roadbook, an editable disclaimer, and a header placeholder. Click "Save as PDF" in the preview, then in the print dialog choose Destination → Save as PDF.
